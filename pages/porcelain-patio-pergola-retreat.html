Porcelain Patio + Pergola Retreat

Oro-Medonte

Design intent: create a refined outdoor room that feels calm, structured, and effortless to use every day.

The Brief

  • Create a level outdoor room connected to the main living area.
  • Maintain a clean, minimal material palette with crisp edges.
  • Add cover for shade and rain without closing the space in.
  • Include lighting that supports evenings without glare.

Design Approach

We organized the layout around a primary dining zone, then wrapped circulation space along the edges to keep movement natural and unobstructed. The pergola aligns with the home’s architecture, framing the outdoor room without overpowering it.

Materials were chosen for visual calm and long-term performance. The porcelain surface offers a consistent tone while keeping joints tight and maintenance simple, and lighting was designed as a soft layer rather than a statement.

Build Details

  • Open-graded base system specified for drainage and frost performance.
  • Large-format porcelain installed with precise joint alignment.
  • Cedar pergola set to align with interior sightlines.
  • Integrated low-voltage lighting for soft evening use.
  • Clean edge restraint and compaction control at transitions.

The Experience

The space feels composed and quiet. Mornings are open and bright, while evenings settle into a softer atmosphere with light and cover balanced carefully.

The pergola keeps the outdoor room comfortable in changing weather, and the porcelain surface stays clean and stable with minimal upkeep.

Highlights

  • Seamless indoor-to-outdoor transitions.
  • Evening lighting layers without glare.
  • Open-graded base for long-term stability.
  • Refined material palette with clean lines.
  • Covered dining zone that feels open.
  • Durable porcelain with low-maintenance upkeep.
